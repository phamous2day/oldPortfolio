E-Yearbook

The International Community School in DeKalb county has been around for 10 years without a yearbook. Being one of the founding members of my own yearbook back in elementary school, I know how pricey color, page count, and printing can be. I figure making a virtual yearbook would be sustainable, cost effective, and one-of-a-kind.

School's Feedback

  • E-Yearbook was made with the help of Georgia Tech. service learning program. I teamed up with students to make a offline-HTML yearbook that stores and structures the layout of the pictures and text. It was wonderful to see a vision become a reality. This initative could not have been done without people to carry on the work, when my AmeriCorps contract finished, so I taught an "After-school E-Yearbook Club." My students learned how to drag and drop images and text into a Java application that sorts the multimedia pieces into a fixed layout.

    Once the E-Yearbook is finished, they can be sold and shared with families overseas. Profit would be used to fund the E-Yearbook Club and equipment that can be shared with the rest of the school to learn multimedia and journalism skills.

    Skills for students

  • Having a 0-dollar budget has been expected from the start for all my AmeriCorps project but that's all the more motivating for me to be tasked with being resourceful with open-source tools and getting help from the community.

    I blue-printed a lot of ideas (if you saw my desk, you'd think I was kid with crayons and access to an infinite canvas), then proto-typed the ideas with the HTML skills I had at the time. Luckily, fate had me cross paths with a service learning program from GT where I had the college students help me further bring my crayon blue-prints into a working Java application and a manual for my after school-students.

    Since class was the first of its kind, I gave a crash-course on what I went through in elememntary school yearbook class. Then, I taught my students a lot of journism skills (shooting video, editing video, photography composition, interviewing skills, etc). Suprisingly, they learned a lot more than I intended and were very ethusiastic with many questions for each class. This was my favorite project during my year as an AmeriCorps VISTA.
